Recently, I've been trying to write for myself more. 
I wrote the first part of this back in February - a series of disjointed thoughts and now discarded statements - and the sentiment still stands now. It is bewildering that I am here! Part of a community of other technical teenagers and young adults from all over the world!
There are teenagers who are innovating and creating novel things and *they are here* with me now, for whatever reason that the universe has decided to come up with.

My parents are not technical people and my siblings have no interest in programming beyond compulsory high school classes.  
I grew up in a smaller city in Australia that is not known for tech, but essentially, the complete opposite. But I also grew up *online*, and much of an impact that has made on me.

I attribute a lot of my current interests to exactly this. I turned the bookish fixations of my youth into a search for a larger community. So, in turn, it surprises me that even now, I've still retained a passion for things younger me was taunted for. 

This doesn't make me any different from who I was before. Surprisingly, or maybe not, upending one's entire life and moving countries to be independent as a young adult didn't exactly lend itself to a fresh start. Is my personality intrinsic? Some might argue that it's a matter of mindset; because surely, those resolute enough can rewrite who they are entirely, but I am, of course, not one of them.

## on travel

During my gap year, I visited New York City a few times, always with different people, and for different reasons. It's a place that scarcely even feels *real* because to me, it was always a city which existed solely in media.

-- this cafe off a side street in lower manhattan. it's 1am and it's bustling with people. i perch on a chair and order a slice of carrot cake; we're here killing time and burning off the post-concert energy

-- the harsh lights of the mta. people around me are drifting off, casually, calmly. in the reflection of the window, i see the anxiety that keeps me awake, and the lack thereof - or is it? is it a sense of security, instead? - that support these friends of mine, people from all over the world, to doze off 

-- wallows in the rain. so fucking surreal. we've already been delayed by four hours but the show must go on. they're playing 'remember when' as the rain starts coming down, the heaviest it's been the whole day. my clothes are soaked, and the crowd around me are ever shifting and shoving through the mud, but i'm still smiling as i raise my gaze to the stormy clouds

-- outside, it's humid as hell. we're somewhere in new jersey - i'm sitting on the single seat on the balcony of our airbnb, watching as droplets pool on the edge of the table. 
   there's one day left. *time passes so fast.* what am i doing?

## on myself

And the reason why I pick New York for this is because I just remember standing in Times Square at midnight, thinking *holy shit* because Times Square is overwhelming in a different way. I will never be 17 years old fucking around on the other side of the world ever again. I don't know. A lot of the time I don't understand how I got here. Sometimes I can't think - a lot of the time I don't feel real, like there's a thin veil separating me from reality. And I've found that the things that ground me most are when I'm in a completely different world.

Because like I said before, I haven't really changed from who I was. I'm an introvert through-and-through. I have terrible social anxiety. I'm still immature in many ways. I wonder a lot whether I was forced to grow up too early. Middle child syndrome - maybe real, probably pseudoscience. I always find myself wondering how to be a human being. How do people *do* things? I get so frustrated because it seems to come so naturally to others. When I'm in a crowd, no one cares who I am and so for a brief moment it doesn't really matter that I think this way. 

I'm not really sure where I'm trying to go here. I think the TLDR is that I don't know who I am and that it's still so hard for me to find out how to get to the stage where I can figure that out. I think that you'd expect that someone in my situation would probably have their shit more together than I do. 

The thing is - and get this - I want to be the kind of person who goes on adventures and journeys to new places. I *want* to see the world. I *want* to be able to live a life that is fulfilling.
